What We Build

Capabilities.

POSM develops experimental hardware and software for quantum, photonic, RF, and sensing systems. Our work focuses on the instrumentation layer between research ideas and working experiments: signal generation, frequency control, optomechanics, electro-optic interfaces, and model-based lab automation.

Bridging ideas and experiments.

Quantum and photonic experiments need more than theory and funding — they need reliable, well-designed instrumentation. POSM builds the hardware and software that sits between a research concept and a functioning experimental system.

Portfolio

Capability areas.

Signal Generation Platforms

Modular mixed-signal and RF boards for waveform generation, routing, filtering, and experiment control. Designed to support flexible laboratory testbeds without locking projects into one fixed architecture.

Frequency-Control Hardware

Timing, modulation, tunability, laser-frequency shifting, and experiment-specific signal conditioning hardware. Built for research workflows where frequency agility and clean control signals matter.

Electro-Optic Assemblies

Compact modulator assemblies, driver interfaces, and optomechanical support hardware for photonic and atomic experiments. Focused on practical integration between optical components, electronics, and mechanical constraints.

Automated Optomechanics

Motorized rotation mounts, polarization and power-control modules, optical monitoring taps, and compact beam-control assemblies. Developed to make optical systems easier to tune, monitor, and repeat.

POSMq Lab Control

POSM's developing model-based experimental-control platform connecting hardware settings, sensor streams, and digital models. Intended to support tuning, drift detection, system monitoring, and automated setup decisions.

Early Access

POSMq Early Access

POSMq is being developed as a model-based lab-control layer for experimental physics systems. We are interested in conversations with research groups, laboratories, and technical partners who may want to pilot future tools for system monitoring, tuning support, drift detection, and automated setup decisions.
