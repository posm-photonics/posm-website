POSM is a student-run engineering research group building experimental photonics, electronics, vacuum, RF, and control hardware. Our partners help move ambitious systems from schematic to lab bench while developing the next generation of experimental engineers.

Start a Partnership → View Our Current Partners
The Work

Your support becomes working hardware.

POSM members do more than simulate systems or study theory. Students design circuits, lay out PCBs, manufacture mechanical assemblies, align optics, write control software, and test complete experimental platforms.

Laser Stabilization and Control

Mixed-signal electronics, RF modulation hardware, FPGA control, and precision optomechanics for atomic and photonic experiments.

Ultra-High-Vacuum Systems

Pumps, valves, gauges, feedthroughs, and custom assemblies supporting POSM's cold-atom research platform.

Automated Optomechanics

Motorized polarization, power-control, rotation, and optical-monitoring hardware designed for repeatable experiments.

Partner contributions are connected to active systems, documented through project updates, and used directly by student engineers.

Partner Value

More than a logo on a page.

POSM partnerships are designed to show what support made possible. Recognition and engagement are tailored to the form and scale of each contribution.

Visible Recognition

  • Featured organization profile and website link on the POSM partners page
  • Recognition in relevant project updates
  • LinkedIn and newsletter acknowledgment
  • Sponsor name or logo on applicable public presentations and demonstrations

Reusable Impact Content

  • Photographs showing the supported project or hardware
  • A concise written update explaining what the contribution enabled
  • A 30–60 second co-branded video documenting the hardware, milestone, or student work their support enabled.
  • Approved media that the partner may repost on its website, LinkedIn, or internal communications

Technical Engagement

  • Invitations to selected project demonstrations
  • Opportunities for company engineers or scientists to give technical talks
  • Mentorship and design-review opportunities
  • Periodic communication with students using the supported equipment, service, or technology

Media, recognition, logo use, and reposting permissions are confirmed with each partner before publication.
